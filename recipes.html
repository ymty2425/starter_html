Double Chocolate Cookies

Origin: Michigan Source: Family Recipe Category: Dessert

My daughter learned to make these cookies at a baking camp at Zingermanns and has tweaked the recipe to fit the taste buds of her siblings. They are extremely sugary so the salt helps to balance it. Note, these cookies are best eaten very quickly.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Packed light or dark brown sugar
  • Large egg
  • Pure vanilla extract
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks (melted)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks

Recipe Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar
  2. Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat well
  3. Add the melted chocolate
  4. In a separate bowl combine the flour, baking soda, cocoa powder and salt
  5. Combine the wet and dry ingredients
  6. Add the unmelted chocolate chunks.
  7. Form 15 cookies and place on a baking sheet.
  8. Cook for 12 to 13 minutes at 350 degrees.

Lemon Herb Grilled Salmon

Origin: Pacific Northwest Source: Grandmother's Cookbook Category: Main Course

This grilled salmon is a summer favorite with its zesty lemon and aromatic herb flavors. The key is to marinate it for at least an hour for the flavors to meld.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Fresh salmon fillets
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh lemon juice
  • Freshly chopped dill
  • Freshly chopped parsley
  • Minced garlic
  • Salt and pepper

Recipe Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix olive oil, lemon juice, dill, parsley, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place salmon fillets in a dish and pour the marinade over them.
  3. Let the salmon marinate in the refrigerator for at least an hour.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  5. Grill the salmon for 5-7 minutes per side, or until it easily flakes with a fork.
  6. Serve with a wedge of lemon and enjoy!

Mango Pineapple Smoothie

Origin: Caribbean Source: Beachside Caf√© Category: Beverage

A tropical blend of ripe mangoes and juicy pineapples makes this smoothie a refreshing drink on a hot day. The addition of creamy yogurt gives it a smooth texture, perfect for a morning boost or afternoon snack.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Ripe mango, peeled and pitted
  • Fresh pineapple chunks
  • Plain or vanilla yogurt
  • Almond milk or any preferred milk
  • Ice cubes
  • Honey or agave syrup (optional)

Recipe Steps

  1. In a blender, combine mango, pineapple, yogurt, and almond milk.
  2. Add ice cubes and blend until smooth.
  3. If desired, add honey or agave syrup to sweeten and blend again until mixed well.
  4. Pour into glasses and serve immediately.
